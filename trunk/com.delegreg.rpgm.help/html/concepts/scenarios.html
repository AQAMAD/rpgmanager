Scenarios

Now, you know what a campaign is all about, let's talk scenarios.

Your PCs have a goal, they need to reach it (or not, though it's rather anticlimatic).

We talked about the step-by-step way to do so, let's talk about the steps.

These steps (or Scenarios) are the books in a saga, the film in a trilogy (or quadrilogy, or sextology..).

Think of your scenarios as the "rather big" accomplishment.

The key to a good scenario is having a good starting point and a good end point.

Too long a scenario ? The players get bored.

Too short a scenario ? The players get bored too.

Your Scenarios should take the players after a "pause" in the storytelling.

Days, Weeks, Months have gone by and the adventurers (or investigators, or whatever) are needed again.

They will again face challenges, battle evil minions (or their commanders as they progress) and save the day.

Make it so they feel their victory (or loss) before your close the scenario, and they'll be hungry for the next one.
